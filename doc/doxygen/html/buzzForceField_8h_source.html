8 /*
9  BuzzForceField :
10  This is a buzzing effect that may be tied to a particular surface to make
11  the surface feel like it is vibrating (buzzing is in direction of
12  surface normal and amplitude gets clipped down to zero as you
13  come off the surface. For accurate simulation of a buzzing surface, the
14  plane should be set to the plane approximation for the surface and the
15  spring constant should match that of the surface if the amplitude is to
16  represent the simulated buzzing amplitude.
17  Strictly speaking, the simulated vibrating plane is shifted by amplitude
18  outwards along normal so force is clipped in the thin layer above
19  the surface at heights between 0 and 2*amplitude. (amplitude is typically
20  on the order of a millimeter)
21  */
